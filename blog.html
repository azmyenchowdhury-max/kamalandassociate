Company Formation in Bangladesh: A Step-by-Step Legal Guide

Adv. Mohammad Mostofa Kamal January 10, 2026
Introduction

Bangladesh is emerging as a major destination for entrepreneurship, manufacturing, and foreign investment. With strong market growth and expanding opportunities, forming a company here can be a high-value strategic move for both local founders and international investors.

Company formation is not merely administrative registration. It creates a separate legal entity, supports limited liability, enables formal contracting and fundraising, and establishes the compliance foundation your business needs to scale safely.

Legal Framework in Bangladesh
1) Companies Act, 1994

This is the principal corporate law framework, with RJSC as the sole registering authority. Common structures include Private Limited Company, Public Limited Company, and One Person Company based on ownership and growth plans.

2) BIDA Act, 2016

BIDA plays a central role for foreign investors, including branch and liaison approvals, expatriate work permit processes, and investment facilitation.

3) Foreign Exchange Regulation Compliance

Foreign-funded entities must route investment through authorized banking channels and preserve proper remittance documentation for future dividend/profit repatriation support.

4) Post-Incorporation Licensing and Tax Setup

RJSC incorporation is only the first step. Businesses also require trade license, e-TIN, VAT/BIN registration, and sectoral approvals where applicable.

Common Legal Issues
1) Name Clearance Rejections

Applications are often delayed due to name similarity or restricted-word conflicts under RJSC naming rules.

2) Defective MoA and AoA

Generic templates can fail to reflect actual business objects and governance needs, creating licensing friction and founder disputes later.

3) Foreign Capital Documentation Errors

Improper inward remittance handling and missing encashment evidence can obstruct registration and future repatriation rights.

4) Missing Shareholders' Agreement

Without tailored shareholder terms, disputes over voting, exits, and profit distribution can quickly escalate.

5) Post-Registration Non-Compliance

Failure to hold AGMs and file annual returns can trigger penalties and eventually jeopardize legal status.

How a Lawyer Can Help
  • Select the right entity model based on ownership, tax exposure, and commercial objectives.
  • Draft robust MoA, AoA, and shareholder documents tailored to your business model.
  • Manage RJSC and BIDA procedures end-to-end to reduce avoidable delay.
  • Secure trade license, tax registration, and sector permits for operational readiness.
  • Support ongoing statutory compliance and governance after incorporation.

Practical Tips for Clients
  • Keep multiple backup names ready before filing for RJSC name clearance.
  • Plan authorized vs paid-up capital carefully to avoid unnecessary stamp duty burden.
  • Secure a valid commercial address with supporting lease documentation early.
  • For foreign shareholders, prioritize proper encashment documentation from day one.

Conclusion

Company formation in Bangladesh is a foundational legal decision that shapes risk, control, and scalability. With proper structuring, compliant documentation, and timely post-registration setup, businesses can avoid avoidable liabilities and build confidently for long-term growth.

FAQs

1. How long does company registration usually take?
With complete documents and proper preparation, RJSC incorporation often completes within approximately 7 to 14 working days.

2. Can foreigners own 100% of a company in Bangladesh?
Yes, in most sectors Bangladesh permits full foreign ownership, subject to regulatory and banking compliance.

3. Is there a minimum paid-up capital requirement?
Local private companies have no strict statutory minimum, though practical and sector-specific requirements may apply in context.

4. What are MoA and AoA?
MoA defines core company identity and objects; AoA governs internal management and shareholder rules.

5. Can business operations begin immediately after RJSC incorporation?
Not fully. Operational readiness also requires local trade licensing and tax registrations.

Key Legal Points
  • Exclusive Registration Authority: RJSC is the formal incorporation body under the Companies Act framework.
  • Separate Legal Personality: Proper incorporation creates liability separation between company and founders.
  • Foreign Investment Documentation: Correct banking routes and encashment records are central to compliance and repatriation.
Practical Insight

Before MoA submission, future-proof the object clause to cover realistic expansion pathways. Post-incorporation amendment can be more time-consuming and administratively burdensome than proper drafting at formation stage.

Commercial Litigation in Bangladesh: A Complete Legal Guide

Adv. Mohammad Kabir January 9, 2026
Introduction

Bangladesh's rise as a major trade, manufacturing, and investment hub has increased the scale and complexity of corporate disputes. When negotiation fails, commercial litigation becomes the legal path for protecting assets, enforcing contracts, and preserving business continuity.

Commercial disputes differ from ordinary civil conflicts because they involve high-value contractual and corporate issues, often with immediate operational and reputational impact. Effective legal strategy requires speed, precision, and a strong command of corporate law.

Legal Framework in Bangladesh
1) Contract Act, 1872

This Act governs contract validity, performance, and breach remedies across vendor agreements, service contracts, non-disclosure clauses, and supply-chain commitments.

2) Companies Act, 1994

Corporate governance, director duties, minority protection, restructuring, and winding-up issues are governed by this Act and often proceed before the Company Bench of the High Court Division.

3) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

The CPC controls procedural stages where commercial matters are tried in lower courts, including pleadings, evidence, and decree execution.

4) Specific Relief Act, 1877

Where monetary damages are insufficient, businesses may seek injunctions, specific performance, and other equitable relief to prevent irreparable commercial loss.

Common Legal Issues
1) Breach of Commercial Contracts

Disputes often arise from delayed delivery, non-payment, non-performance, or quality failure in business agreements.

2) Shareholder and Director Disputes

Minority oppression claims, governance deadlocks, and fiduciary breaches can destabilize company operations and require urgent intervention.

3) Partnership Disputes

Profit sharing, dissolution terms, and asset valuation disagreements frequently trigger contested civil and commercial proceedings.

4) Intellectual Property Infringement

Trademark misuse, design copying, and software/content infringement can justify urgent injunction strategy.

5) Joint Venture and FDI Conflicts

Control rights, regulatory approvals, and profit repatriation conflicts are common in cross- border investment structures.

Commercial litigation in Bangladesh demands legal precision, timely intervention, and contract- driven strategy. With strong documentation, early injunction planning, and experienced counsel, businesses can manage dispute risk while protecting long-term growth objectives.

FAQs

1. Civil vs commercial litigation: what is the difference?
Commercial litigation centers on business and corporate disputes, while ordinary civil cases often involve personal rights and non-business conflicts.

2. Can minority shareholders sue majority owners?
Yes, where statutory thresholds and factual grounds are met, minority-oppression remedies may be sought under company law before competent courts.

3. How long do commercial disputes take in court?
Timelines vary by forum and complexity; ADR and arbitration often resolve disputes faster than fully contested court proceedings.

4. Are foreign commercial judgments enforceable in Bangladesh?
Enforceability depends on reciprocity status and procedural compliance under applicable law.

5. Can contract disputes be resolved through arbitration?
Yes. Where arbitration clauses exist, parties are generally required to pursue arbitration under the Arbitration Act framework.

Key Legal Points
  • Jurisdiction: Forum selection depends on claim type, value, and whether company-law issues invoke High Court company jurisdiction.
  • Injunction Leverage: Early interim relief can preserve disputed assets and prevent irreversible commercial harm.
  • Limitation Discipline: Delay in filing may legally bar otherwise valid claims.
Practical Insight

Before suing a defaulting vendor or partner, conduct asset and solvency checks. A favorable decree has limited value if enforcement targets hold no recoverable assets.

Civil Litigation in Bangladesh: A Complete Guide to Resolving Disputes

Adv. Mohammad Kabir January 8, 2026
Introduction

As commercial activity, property transactions, and cross-sector investments accelerate in Bangladesh, disputes are becoming more frequent and complex. When negotiation fails, civil litigation becomes the formal mechanism for enforcing rights, recovering losses, and protecting legal interests.

Civil litigation differs from criminal law by addressing conflicts between private parties, businesses, and institutions. For investors, property holders, and business operators, effective dispute strategy requires procedural discipline, documentary strength, and experienced legal representation.

Legal Framework in Bangladesh
1) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC)

The CPC is the principal procedural framework governing suit institution, pleadings, evidence, trial stages, decree, and appellate remedies across civil courts.

2) Specific Relief Act, 1877

This statute supports non-monetary remedies such as injunctions and specific performance in cases where compensation alone is inadequate.

3) Contract Act, 1872 and Transfer of Property Act, 1882

These laws define enforceability of agreements, breach consequences, and rights in sale, lease, mortgage, and transfer of immovable property.

4) Civil Court Hierarchy

Disputes usually begin in subordinate courts based on pecuniary jurisdiction and can progress on appeal through District Courts to the High Court Division and Appellate Division.

Common Legal Issues
1) Land and Property Disputes

Title defects, possession disputes, forged documents, boundary conflicts, and inheritance partition matters are among the most frequent civil claims.

2) Breach of Contract Claims

Businesses and individuals often seek damages or specific performance where contractual promises are violated.

3) Injunction and Status Quo Relief

Temporary injunctions are commonly used to prevent irreversible loss while full trial is pending.

4) Commercial and Corporate Litigation

Shareholder conflicts, JV disputes, and intellectual property-linked civil claims require both procedural and commercial strategy.

5) Money Recovery Suits

Where specialized banking forums do not apply, standard civil money suits remain the key recovery path for unpaid dues.

How a Lawyer Can Help
  • Draft technically sound plaints, written statements, and interlocutory applications.
  • Secure urgent interim orders, including injunctions and status quo directives.
  • Conduct witness examination and evidence management throughout trial stages.
  • Handle appeals and revisions where lower-court findings are legally or factually flawed.
  • Negotiate strategic settlement during mandatory mediation under Section 89A CPC.

Practical Tips for Clients
  • Track limitation periods carefully; time-barred suits are dismissed regardless of merit.
  • Preserve originals of deeds, contracts, payment proofs, and correspondence.
  • Never ignore summons; ex parte decrees can be passed in your absence.
  • Evaluate litigation economics against settlement opportunities.

Conclusion

Civil litigation in Bangladesh is procedure-intensive and often time-consuming, but strong preparation materially improves outcomes. With timely action, disciplined documentation, and strategic legal representation, parties can navigate disputes effectively and secure meaningful relief.

FAQs

1. How long does a civil suit usually take in Bangladesh?
Timelines vary by court load and case complexity; many contested matters in lower courts can take several years, with additional time if appeals follow.

2. What is an injunction?
An injunction is a court order restraining or compelling action to prevent injustice while a dispute is pending or after decree.

3. What is the limitation period for civil claims?
Limitation depends on claim type under applicable law; for example, many contract claims are filed within three years from breach.

4. Is mediation mandatory in civil cases?
Section 89A CPC integrates court-directed mediation in civil proceedings before full trial continuation.

5. What if the judgment debtor does not comply with decree?
The decree-holder can initiate execution proceedings for attachment, sale, or other enforcement relief recognized by law.

Key Legal Points
  • CPC Control: Civil procedure governs every litigation stage from filing to execution and appeal.
  • Limitation Discipline: Claims filed beyond statutory limits risk immediate dismissal.
  • Mediation Integration: ADR is a formal part of the civil dispute lifecycle in Bangladesh.
Practical Insight

Before filing a property suit, conduct comprehensive title verification at relevant registry and land offices. Early due diligence can prevent years of litigation over documents that may not support a sustainable claim.

Navigating Banking Litigation in Bangladesh: A Comprehensive Guide for Businesses and Borrowers

Adv. Mustafa Kamal Chowdhury January 5, 2026
Introduction

The financial sector is central to Bangladesh's economic growth, supporting industrial expansion, international trade, and personal credit markets. As lending volume and transaction complexity increase, disputes between banks, NBFIs, borrowers, guarantors, and investors are becoming more frequent and more consequential.

Banking litigation can expose clients to account freezes, CIB consequences, property auction, and parallel criminal proceedings. Effective defense and recovery strategy requires not only legal precision but also commercial judgment aligned with financial survival and business continuity.

Legal Framework in Bangladesh
1) Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003

This statute creates specialized debt recovery courts with exclusive jurisdiction for bank-led loan recovery suits. Summary-style procedures are designed for faster recovery compared to ordinary civil litigation.

2) Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881

Section 138 criminalizes cheque dishonour and is frequently used alongside civil recovery actions, increasing pressure in default disputes.

3) Bank Company Act, 1991 and Bangladesh Bank Circulars

Regulatory directives on classification, rescheduling, and financial conduct can directly affect litigation outcomes. Non-compliance by institutions often triggers higher-court challenges.

4) Contract Act, 1872 and Transfer of Property Act, 1882

Guarantees, security documents, and mortgage enforceability are rooted in these foundational laws and often become critical in contested recovery proceedings.

Related Service

Common Legal Issues
1) Loan Defaults and Artha Rin Suits

Disputes often arise over interest computation, classification of default, and compliance with restructuring directives.

2) Parallel Cheque Dishonour Cases

Banks may pursue Section 138 criminal proceedings based on security cheque dishonour while civil recovery continues, creating dual-track legal exposure.

3) Section 12 Auction Disputes

Borrowers challenge auctions on valuation irregularities, inadequate notice, and non-compliant process affecting mortgaged property transfers.

4) Guarantor and Director Liability

Guarantor obligations are generally co-extensive, enabling lenders to proceed against personal assets in corporate default scenarios.

5) Trade Finance and LC Conflicts

Document fraud allegations, UCP 600 interpretation disputes, and fund-release refusals can halt supply chains and require urgent injunctive relief.

  • Seek immediate legal intervention on auction notices before third-party transfer occurs.

Conclusion

Banking litigation in Bangladesh is fast-moving and high-stakes, with strong statutory tools in favor of recovery. However, lenders must still comply with procedural and regulatory requirements. With timely legal action and disciplined documentation strategy, borrowers and businesses can challenge unjust claims and protect long-term financial stability.

FAQs

1. What happens if I lose in Artha Rin Adalat?
The decree can be executed against assets through recovery procedures, and further action may follow depending on compliance and conduct.

2. Can I go to jail for failing to repay a bank loan?
Pure civil default does not automatically mean imprisonment, but cheque dishonour convictions under Section 138 can carry criminal penalties.

3. How long does a bank have to file a cheque dishonour case?
The process depends on strict NI Act notice and filing timelines after dishonour.

4. What is a writ petition in banking disputes?
It is a constitutional remedy before the High Court Division to challenge unlawful administrative actions affecting legal rights.

5. Can a banking dispute be settled out of court?
Yes. Mediation and negotiated settlement are widely used and can be formalized as binding outcomes.

Key Legal Points
  • Specialized Courts: Debt recovery suits by financial institutions are filed in Artha Rin Adalat.
  • Dual Exposure: Borrowers may face civil recovery and criminal cheque litigation simultaneously.
  • Mediation Requirement: Courts are expected to attempt settlement before full contested trial progression.
Practical Insight

When finalizing restructuring or one-time settlement terms, explicitly require withdrawal of pending cheque dishonour matters. Without this, criminal exposure may continue despite a civil financial settlement.

Banking Litigation in Bangladesh: A Complete Legal Guide

Adv. Mostofa Kamal January 4, 2026
Introduction

In Bangladesh's expanding economy, financial transactions power business growth, trade finance, and household credit. But where credit volume increases, disputes around default, repayment, collateral, and recoverability also rise rapidly. Banking litigation has therefore become one of the most commercially significant legal areas in the country.

These disputes can involve banks, NBFIs, corporate borrowers, guarantors, and individual clients. Outcomes can directly affect liquidity, asset ownership, and business continuity. Timely legal strategy is essential for both institutions pursuing recovery and borrowers defending against coercive action.

Legal Framework in Bangladesh
1) Artha Rin Adalat Ain, 2003

This is the principal debt recovery framework for banks and financial institutions. It creates specialized Artha Rin courts with exclusive jurisdiction and expedited procedures for loan recovery cases.

2) Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 (Section 138)

Section 138 addresses cheque dishonour for insufficient funds and is frequently used in parallel with civil recovery actions. It has criminal consequences, including potential imprisonment and financial penalties.

3) Bank Company Act, 1991

This Act supports regulatory oversight by Bangladesh Bank and influences disputes around loan classification, rescheduling, and compliance with binding circulars and directives.

4) Contract Act, 1872 and Transfer of Property Act, 1882

Underlying loan agreements, guarantees, and mortgage enforcement depend substantially on general contract and property law principles.

Related Service

Explore our business-focused advisory and dispute support for banking and commercial transactions.

Explore Corporate and Commercial Services
Common Legal Issues
1) Loan Defaults and Artha Rin Recovery Suits

Disputes often center on principal calculations, compound interest treatment, and whether lenders complied with restructuring and regulatory prerequisites.

2) Cheque Dishonour Cases

Section 138 cases can proceed in parallel with civil debt recovery. Defenses frequently involve statutory notice defects and procedural non-compliance.

3) LC and Trade Finance Conflicts

Cross-border document discrepancies, alleged fraud, and refusal to honor credit instruments can trigger urgent injunction and writ strategy.

4) Auction of Mortgaged Property
