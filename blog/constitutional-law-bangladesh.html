Key Legal Points
  • The Constitution of Bangladesh (1972) is the supreme law — any law inconsistent with it is void to the extent of the inconsistency.
  • Part III of the Constitution (Articles 26–47A) guarantees fundamental rights enforceable directly through the courts.
  • Article 102 empowers the High Court Division to issue writs of certiorari, mandamus, prohibition, habeas corpus, and quo warranto.
  • The Supreme Court of Bangladesh — comprising the High Court Division and the Appellate Division — is the ultimate guardian of the Constitution.
